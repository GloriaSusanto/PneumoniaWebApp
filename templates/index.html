Pneumonia is a common respiratory infection characterized by inflammation of the air sacs in one or both lungs. It typically presents with symptoms such as cough, fever, difficulty breathing, and chest pain. Pneumonia can lead to a decline in respiratory function, which may hinder daily activities. Although the exact cause of pneumonia can vary, it is often triggered by bacterial, viral, or fungal infections. While treatments such as antibiotics or antiviral medications are available for pneumonia, they primarily aim to manage symptoms and combat the underlying infection rather than providing a definitive cure.
